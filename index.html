STEM in Robotics

  • Integrated Learning: Unlike learning subjects in isolation, robotics connects them; for example, engineering the structure, coding the movement (Technology), calculating angles (Math), and using sensors (Science).
  • Educational Goal: It fosters skills like creativity, debugging, and logical thinking by turning theoretical knowledge into functional, physical, or virtual machines.
  • Application: It is widely used in education to teach coding, AI, and engineering principles through interactive projects like creating robots that navigate, pick up objects, or follow lines.
  • Future-Ready Skills:STEM robotics prepares individuals for future careers by developing critical skills in demand within high-tech industries.
